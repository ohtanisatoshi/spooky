I fed it with a few crumbs from a biscuit; by degrees it revived; its warm fluttering heart beat against me; I cannot tell why I detail this trifling incident but the scene is still before me; the snow clad fields seen through the silvered trunks of the beeches, the brook, in days of happiness alive with sparkling waters, now choked by ice the leafless trees fantastically dressed in hoar frost the shapes of summer leaves imaged by winter's frozen hand on the hard ground the dusky sky, drear cold, and unbroken silence while close in my bosom, my feathered nursling lay warm, and safe, speaking its content with a light chirp painful reflections thronged, stirring my brain with wild commotion cold and death like as the snowy fields was all earth misery stricken the life tide of the inhabitants why should I oppose the cataract of destruction that swept us away?
