My first moment of positive inspiration must be dated from that ever memorable epoch, when the brilliant conductor of the "Gad Fly," in the intervals of the important process just mentioned, recited aloud, before a conclave of our apprentices, an inimitable poem in honor of the "Only Genuine Oil of Bob," so called from its talented inventor, my father, and for which effusion the editor of the "Fly" was remunerated with a regal liberality by the firm of Thomas Bob and company, merchant barbers.
